# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and maintainers of [Open Source Kigali](https://github.com/Open-Source-Kigali) pledge to make participation in our community a respectful, safe, and welcoming experience for everyone, regardless of experience level, background, identity, personal appearance, nationality, ethnicity, disability, religion, or level of technical knowledge.

We are committed to fostering an open and collaborative environment focused on learning, knowledge sharing, and building impactful open source software together.

---

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment include:

* Being respectful and constructive in discussions and code reviews
* Welcoming newcomers and helping others learn
* Giving and receiving feedback professionally
* Assuming good intent while still communicating clearly
* Respecting differing viewpoints and experiences
* Focusing on what is best for the community and project
* Using inclusive and professional language
* Taking responsibility for mistakes and learning from them

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

* Harassment, discrimination, or hateful conduct of any kind
* Personal attacks, insults, or derogatory comments
* Trolling, intimidation, or deliberately disruptive behavior
* Sexualized language or unwanted advances
* Publishing private information without permission
* Repeatedly ignoring project guidelines or maintainer decisions
* Spam, self-promotion unrelated to the project, or malicious activity
* Any conduct that would be considered inappropriate in a professional environment

---

## Collaboration Guidelines

To help keep the project productive and maintainable:

* Keep pull requests focused and reasonably scoped
* Be open to feedback and requested changes
* Discuss major architectural or feature proposals before implementation
* Respect code review decisions made by maintainers
* Prefer constructive technical discussion over arguments
* Avoid hostile or dismissive communication, especially toward beginners

For practical contribution guidelines (setup, branching, commit conventions, and the pull request flow), see [CONTRIBUTING.md](./CONTRIBUTING.md).

Disagreements are normal in software development, but they should always remain respectful and centered on the technical problem being solved.

---

## Maintainer Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for:

* Clarifying and enforcing community standards
* Reviewing contributions fairly and respectfully
* Taking appropriate action in response to unacceptable behavior
* Prioritizing the long-term health of the project and community
* Creating an environment where contributors feel comfortable participating

Maintainers reserve the right to remove, edit, or reject contributions, comments, issues, discussions, or other interactions that violate this Code of Conduct.

---

## Reporting Issues

If you experience or witness behavior that violates this Code of Conduct, please report it to the maintainers by opening an issue on the repository:

* [GitHub Issues](https://github.com/Open-Source-Kigali/oskbackend/issues)

When reporting an issue, include:

* A description of what happened
* Links or screenshots if relevant
* Any additional context that may help resolve the issue

All reports will be reviewed respectfully and handled as confidentially as possible.

---

## Enforcement

Project maintainers may take any action they deem appropriate in response to violations of this Code of Conduct, including:

* Warning the contributor
* Removing comments or content
* Temporarily restricting participation
* Permanently banning individuals from the community

Enforcement decisions are made at the discretion of the maintainers and are intended to protect the health and safety of the community.

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all project spaces, including:

* GitHub repositories
* Pull requests and code reviews
* Issues
* Events or meetups associated with the project

It also applies when an individual is representing Open Source Kigali in public spaces.
